The music is free.
Give what moves you.

On long silent retreats at Spirit Rock, Sarah-Marie Hopf — wisdom teacher, meditation teacher, dharma musician — began receiving chants. Not composing them. Receiving them. Simple, whole, insistent. She brought them to her friend Nathan Vanderpool and the Secret Chord Collective, who gave them arrangement, voice, and recorded life.

There is an old tradition of songs that carry what words alone cannot — spontaneous songs of realization sung by Indian and Tibetan masters, in which the teaching arrives through music because the mind has finally gone quiet enough to receive it. These chants come from that same place. They emerged in stillness and they work through repetition: something loosens the more you sing them.

In the spirit of dana — the Buddhist practice of freely giving and freely receiving — the music asks nothing in return. The teachings were offered freely at Spirit Rock. We offer these chants the same way.

If they have meant something to you, and you feel moved to give, you can support the making of the next batch here.
